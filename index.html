About Me

Hi! I am in my first year as a PhD student, pursuing the Ph.D. degree in an interdisciplinary program at the School of Design, Shanghai Jiao Tong University. I previously received my M.S. in Industrial Design Engineering from SJTU and my B.S. in Mechanical Engineering from Hunan University.

I aim to leverage design-driven innovation to influence human attitudes, emotions, and behaviors, creating physical objects based on theoretical insights to shape tangible interactions, foster richer, more meaningful real-world interactions in daily life.

Currently, I focus on live streaming and social media as critical context, exploring to design a series of tangible interactive products to address the negative emotional and behavioral impacts of digital interactions, ultimately enhancing human well-being by reducing cognitive dissonance and attentional imbalance.

Projects

Physical Environment and Light Interaction

This project is on the interaction between humans and their physical environments, particularly how natural light affects attitudes, emotions, and behaviors. In this area, I have developed AI-integrated, computationally driven programmable daylight regulation components through innovative participatory design processes, aiming to shape human-environment interactions while addressing aesthetic and comfort needs.

As an extension of my work on human-environment interaction, I also contributed to a project exploring the design of 3D-printed components with fluidic tunable thermal conductivity for applications such as ice therapy wearables and containers.

Digital Environment and Social Interaction

The project is on human interactions in digital environments, particularly in the context of live streaming. I aim to optimize social performance and interface design through data-driven methods, enhancing user experience and promoting consumer behavior.

This line of research is still ongoing
