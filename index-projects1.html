User-involved Daylighting Regulation
Focusing on user-participatory design for indoor visual comfort via AI-assisted interactive systems and daylight shading simulation, validated through prototypes and experiments, proposed combined method and system.
Consumer experience of Daylight in public space
Treating users in public spaces as consumers of the commodity known as the indoor environment, the study developed a model based on the ABC Model to uncovered a positive influence of daylighting on user behavior, efficiency, and satisfaction, while identifying a negative impact of glare.
Research on Parametric Design Cases of Daylight Regulation: A User Perspective
Based on the classification of design inspirations in the research of human settlement light environment, case analyses of three representative shading studies are carried out. It clarifies the advantages and significance of the existing design methods and strategies, and provides enlightenment from three aspects: seeking design inspiration, empowering users' discourse power, and thinking about the entire design process.
Parametric design strategies for user-centered products
Based on design thinking, proposed a reverse design strategy to assist designers in developing light related design based on functional attributes, employed observation and interview methods, combined with affinity diagrams to identify user needs and created user personas.
Programmable Heat Transfer
Thermaterial uses fluidic composites with tunable thermal properties to enable heat behavior without continuous energy input.
